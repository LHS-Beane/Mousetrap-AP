Unit 2: Force & Dynamics

Understanding the causes of motion. Newton's Laws, Free Body Diagrams, Friction, and Gravitational Fields.

Core Concepts

[Image of free body diagram physics] 1. Free Body Diagrams (FBD)

The most critical skill in Unit 2. Isolate the object and draw vectors for every force acting on it (Gravity, Normal, Tension, Friction).

2. Newton's Second Law

Acceleration is proportional to Net Force and inversely proportional to Mass.

$$\vec{a} = \frac{\sum \vec{F}}{m}$$

This is a vector equation: Solve for x and y separately.

[Image of static vs kinetic friction graph] 3. Friction ($f_s$ vs $f_k$)

Static: Prevents motion. Variable force ($\le \mu_s F_n$).

Kinetic: Opposes sliding motion. Constant force ($= \mu_k F_n$).

[Image of box on inclined plane physics diagram] 4. Inclined Planes

Tilt your axis! Set the x-axis parallel to the ramp.

  • $F_{gx} = mg \sin\theta$ (Slides down)
  • $F_{gy} = mg \cos\theta$ (Pushes into ramp)

Dynamics Equations

Newton's 2nd Law

$$\sum \vec{F} = m\vec{a}$$

Friction Force

$$|\vec{F}_f| \le \mu |\vec{F}_n|$$

Gravitational Force

$$|\vec{F}_g| = mg$$

Worked Example: The Pull

Problem:

A 10 kg box is pulled across a rough floor by a rope at a 30Â° angle above the horizontal. The tension in the rope is 50 N. The coefficient of kinetic friction ($\mu_k$) is 0.2. Calculate the acceleration of the box.


Step 1: Draw the FBD

Forces: Gravity (down), Normal (up), Tension (up-right), Friction (left).

Step 2: Break Vectors into Components

Tension ($T$) needs splitting:

$T_x = 50 \cos(30^\circ) \approx 43.3 \text{ N}$

$T_y = 50 \sin(30^\circ) = 25 \text{ N}$

Step 3: Sum of Forces (Y-Direction)

The box is not flying or sinking, so $a_y = 0$.

$\sum F_y = F_n + T_y - F_g = 0$

$F_n = mg - T_y = (10)(9.8) - 25 = 73 \text{ N}$

Step 4: Calculate Friction

$F_f = \mu_k F_n = (0.2)(73) = 14.6 \text{ N}$

Step 5: Sum of Forces (X-Direction)

$\sum F_x = T_x - F_f = ma_x$

$43.3 - 14.6 = 10 a$

$28.7 = 10 a \rightarrow \mathbf{a = 2.87 \text{ m/s}^2}$

Unit Labs & Investigations

1. Determining the Coefficient of Friction ($\mu$)

Objective: Experimentally determine $\mu_s$ (static) and $\mu_k$ (kinetic) between a wood block and a track.

Method A: The Drag (Horizontal)

Pull a block of mass $m$ at a constant velocity using a Force Sensor.

  • Since $a=0$, $\sum F = 0$, therefore $F_{pull} = F_{friction}$.
  • Data Collection: Add mass to the block (increasing $F_n$) and measure the new $F_f$.
  • Analysis: Plot $F_f$ (y-axis) vs. $F_n$ (x-axis).
  • Result: The slope of the line is $\mu_k$.

Method B: The Slip (Incline)

Place the block on a ramp and slowly increase the angle $\theta$ until it just starts to slide.

  • At the moment of slip: $F_{gx} = f_s(max)$.
  • $mg \sin\theta = \mu_s (mg \cos\theta)$.
  • Result: Mass cancels out! $\mu_s = \tan(\theta)$.
2. Atwood Machines (Systems)

Objective: Verify Newton's 2nd Law for a system of connected objects.

Setup: Two masses ($M > m$) connected by a string over a pulley.

Theory: Treat the system as one object bending around the corner.

$$a_{sys} = \frac{\text{Net Force}}{\text{Total Mass}} = \frac{Mg - mg}{M + m}$$
