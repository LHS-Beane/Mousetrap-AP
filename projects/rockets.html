Project: Water Rocketry

Apply Newton's 3rd Law and kinematics to design a pressurized launch vehicle. Maximize height and analyze the initial thrust phase.

Project Introduction

The objective of this project is to design and build a water rocket that achieves stable flight and maximum altitude. You will not only construct the rocket but also perform a detailed physics analysis of its motion during the first 0.2 seconds of launch (the thrust phase).

[Image of Water rocket free body diagram]

Physics Theory

  • Newton's 3rd Law: The rocket pushes water down; the water pushes the rocket up.
  • Impulse & Momentum: The change in momentum of the water ($\Delta p$) creates the impulse ($J$) on the rocket.
  • Projectile Motion: Once the water is exhausted, the rocket enters free fall (neglecting air resistance).

Mathematical Analysis (23 pts)

This is the core physics component. You must analyze the motion during the first 0.2 seconds (approximate thrust phase) and the total flight.

Requirement: Present data in a table, show equations, and provide a sample calculation for each.

1. Maximum Height ($y_{max}$)

Method: Timed Flight

$$y_{max} = \frac{1}{2} g \left( \frac{t_{total}}{2} \right)^2$$

Assumption: Time up equals time down (if landing on flat ground).

2. Acceleration ($a_y$)

Find average acceleration during the first 0.2s.

$$y = y_0 + v_{iy}t + \frac{1}{2}a_y t^2$$

If you can measure height at t=0.2s using video analysis, solve for $a$. Otherwise, estimate using force analysis.

3. Velocity at t = 0.2s

This is the "Initial Velocity" of the coasting phase.

$$v_f = v_i + a_y t$$

Use the acceleration calculated in Step 2 and $t=0.2$.

4. Force Exerted ($F_{thrust}$)

Average force during the first 0.2s.

$$\sum F = F_{thrust} - mg = ma$$

$$F_{thrust} = m(a + g)$$

5. Impact Velocity

Final velocity before striking the ground.

$$v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2g(y_{max})$$

Fall from max height. $v_i$ at peak is 0.
