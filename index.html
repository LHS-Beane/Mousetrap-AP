Project Objective

Your challenge is to design, build, and test a vehicle powered *only* by the energy stored in one or two standard mousetraps. You will then analyze the physics of your car's motion in a formal lab report. This project will test your understanding of kinematics, Newton's laws, energy, and friction.

Project Components & Grade

Your total project grade is worth 145 points, broken down as follows:

  • The Car (65 points):
    • In-Class Work (15 points)
    • Performance/Distance (50 points)
  • The Lab Report (80 points):
    • Data analysis and write-up.

The Car: Rules & Performance

This section covers the requirements for your car's construction and the performance goals you need to meet.

Group Size & Distance Goal

  • Groups of 1-2 students: Your car must travel a minimum distance of 10.0 meters.
  • Groups of 3 students: Your car must travel a minimum distance of 15.0 meters.

Car Construction Rules

  1. Power: The car must be powered by 1 or 2 standard mousetraps (provided). You may not use any other energy source (e.g., batteries, CO2, rubber bands not part of the trap).
  2. Materials: The car must be completely homemade.
    • NO pre-made kits, Legos, K'nex, Erector sets, etc.
    • You MAY use raw materials like wood, metal, plastic, glue, string, and bottle caps/CDs for wheels.
  3. Wheels: The car must have at least 3 wheels.
  4. Launch: The car must start from a stationary position. You may hold it, but not push it, to start.

The Lab Report Requirements

Your lab report must be formatted professionally and include the following 8 sections. All data should be clearly presented and calculations shown.

  1. Title Page: Project title, your name(s), course name, and date.
  2. Introduction: What is the purpose of this project? What are the engineering challenges? What physics principles govern the car's motion? (Discuss potential & kinetic energy, work, friction, and Newton's Laws).
  3. Materials List & Drawings: A bulleted list of all materials used. A clear, labeled drawing (a "blueprint" or schematic) of your car's design.
  4. Data (10 Trials):
    • You must measure and record the mass of your car (in kg).
    • Create a neat data table for 10 official trials.
    • Data to collect for each trial:
      • Distance to "Snap" (d1) (m)
      • Time to "Snap" (t1) (s)
      • Total Distance (Stop) (dtotal) (m)
      • Total Time (Stop) (ttotal) (s)
  5. Calculations:
    • Include a table of your calculated values for all 10 trials, plus the averages.
    • Show one complete sample calculation for each:
      • Average Velocity at Snap (vsnap)
      • Acceleration *during* Snap (a1)
      • Acceleration *after* Snap (a2)
      • Frictional Force (Ff)
  6. Results & Discussion: Discuss your results. Was your data consistent? Reference the physics: How did your design try to maximize a1 and minimize a2? Where did Ff come from? Discuss energy transformations. What were the sources of error?
  7. Conclusion: Summarize your key findings. If you could rebuild, what specific changes would you make? Why?
  8. Sources: Cite a minimum of 3 sources in a standard science paper format.

Physics & Math Hints

Model your car's motion in two separate phases. Use these formulas for your "Calculations" section.

Phase 1: Acceleration (From t=0 to t1)

The trap is actively pulling the car.

  • The car starts from rest, so vi = 0.
  • It travels a distance d1 in a time t1.
  • Find Acceleration (a1): d1 = vi t1 + ½ a1 t12 Since vi = 0, this simplifies to: a1 = (2d1) / t12
  • Find Velocity at Snap (vsnap): vsnap = vi + a1 t1 This simplifies to: vsnap = a1 t1

Phase 2: Coasting (From t1 to ttotal)

The trap has finished pulling, and the car is coasting to a stop due to friction.

  • The car's "initial velocity" for this phase is vsnap from Phase 1.
  • The car's "final velocity" is vf = 0 (it stops).
  • The time for this phase is t2 = ttotal - t1.
  • Find Coasting Acceleration (a2): vf = vsnap + a2 t2 This simplifies to: a2 = (-vsnap) / t2 (This value should be negative!)
  • Find Frictional Force (Ff): Fnet = ma This simplifies to: Ff = m · a2
