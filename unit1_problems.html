Multiple Choice

1. A position vs time graph is a straight line with positive slope. What is true?

  1. The cart’s speed is increasing.
  2. The cart is moving at constant velocity.
  3. The cart’s acceleration is constant and nonzero.
  4. The cart is at rest.
Show Answer
Correct Answer: B
A straight line on an $x$–$t$ graph means constant velocity, so acceleration is zero.

2. An object’s velocity-time graph is a horizontal line at $v = -3\text{ m/s}$. What is true?

  1. The object is slowing down.
  2. The object has constant negative acceleration.
  3. The object moves at constant speed in the negative direction.
  4. The object is stationary.
Show Answer
Correct Answer: C
A horizontal $v$–$t$ line means constant velocity.

3. A projectile at its highest point has:

  1. $v_x=0$, $v_y=0$
  2. $v_x=0$, $v_y\neq 0$
  3. $v_x\neq 0$, $v_y=0$
  4. $v_x\neq 0$, $v_y\neq 0$
Show Answer
Correct Answer: C
At the peak, vertical velocity is zero but horizontal velocity is unchanged.

4. A student walks 3 m east and then 4 m north. What is the displacement?

  1. 1 m
  2. 5 m
  3. 7 m
  4. 12 m
Show Answer
Correct Answer: B
Use Pythagorean theorem: $\sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5$ m.

Short Free Response

5. An object’s velocity increases linearly from $2$ m/s to $6$ m/s over 4 seconds.

(a) Find acceleration.

(b) Determine displacement from the graph.

(c) Verify using $x = v_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$.

Show Solution
(a) $a = \frac{6 - 2}{4} = 1\ \text{m/s}^2$

(b) Area under $v$–$t$ graph = average velocity × time = $4 \times 4 = 16$ m

(c) $x = (2)(4) + \frac12 (1)(4^2) = 8 + 8 = 16$ m

6. Two cars begin at the same position. Car A moves at $10$ m/s. Car B starts from rest and accelerates at $2\ \text{m/s}^2$.

(a) Write expressions for $x_A(t)$ and $x_B(t)$.

(b) When does B catch A?

(c) Sketch a position–time graph.

Show Solution
$x_A = 10t$
$x_B = \frac12 (2)t^2 = t^2$

Solve $10t = t^2 \Rightarrow t(t - 10) = 0$ → $t = 10$ s
Car B catches A at 10 seconds.

7. A ball is dropped from rest. (a) Velocity after 3 s? (b) Distance fallen? (c) Sketch $v$–$t$ graph.

Show Solution
(a) $v = gt = (9.8)(3) = 29.4$ m/s

(b) $y = \frac12 gt^2 = 0.5(9.8)(9) = 44.1$ m

(c) Straight line from 0 to 29.4 with slope $g$.

Long Free Response

8. A constant-velocity buggy’s $x$–$t$ graph is a straight line. Write a CER response evaluating: “The buggy always moves at exactly the same velocity.”

Show Solution
Claim: The buggy moves at approximately constant velocity, but not perfectly constant.

Evidence: The $x$–$t$ graph is nearly a straight line, but data points scatter slightly around the best-fit line.

Reasoning: A straight line indicates constant velocity because the slope (velocity) does not change. The scatter indicates measurement uncertainty or small variations in the buggy’s motor output.

9. A projectile is launched from 1.0 m at $8.0$ m/s at $40^\circ$.

(a) Find $v_x$ and $v_y$.

(b) Write $x(t)$ and $y(t)$.

(c) Find maximum height.

(d) Time of flight.

(e) Range.

Show Solution
Components: $v_x = 8\cos40^\circ = 6.13$ m/s
$v_y = 8\sin40^\circ = 5.14$ m/s

Position functions: $x = v_x t$
$y = 1.0 + v_y t - \tfrac12 g t^2$

Max height: occurs at $t = v_y/g = 0.525$ s
$h = 1.0 + (5.14)(0.525) - 0.5(9.8)(0.525^2) = 2.35$ m

Total time: solve $y(t)=0$ → $t = 1.17$ s

Range: $x = v_x t = 6.13(1.17) = 7.17$ m
