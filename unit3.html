Work, Energy, and Power (CED Topics 3.1–3.5)

Home

Unit 3 shifts the focus from forces to energy. You should be able to:

  • Use kinetic, gravitational, and elastic energy to describe motion.
  • Connect force and displacement to work.
  • Interpret and build energy bar charts + graphs.
  • Apply conservation of energy to explain motion.
  • Compute and compare power as the rate of energy transfer.

Kinetic energy describes the energy of motion:

$$K = \tfrac12 m v^2$$

  • Depends on mass and speed only.
  • If speed doubles → kinetic energy becomes 4× larger.
  • K is always positive; direction of velocity doesn’t matter.

Quick Check

A car speeds up from 10 m/s to 20 m/s. By what factor does $K$ increase?

$$K \propto v^2 \Rightarrow (20/10)^2 = 4.$$ K increases by 4×.

Work is energy transferred by a force acting through displacement.

$$W = F d \cos\theta$$

$$W = \int F(x)\, dx \quad \text{(area under the } F\!-\!x \text{ graph)}$$

  • $W > 0$ → system gains energy.
  • $W < 0$ → system loses energy.
  • If force ⟂ displacement → $W = 0$.

Quick Check

Force increases linearly from 0→10 N over 0→2 m. Compute the work.

Triangle area: $$W = \tfrac12 (2)(10)=10\text{ J}.$$

Gravitational Potential Energy

$$U_g = mgh$$

Elastic Potential Energy

$$U_s = \tfrac12 k x^2$$

  • Potential energy is stored in interactions, not objects.
  • The zero level for $U_g$ is arbitrary but must be consistent.

Quick Check

How much gravitational energy is gained by lifting 2 kg up 1.5 m?

$$\Delta U = mgh = (2)(9.8)(1.5) \approx 29\text{ J}.$$

$$K_i + U_i = K_f + U_f$$

$$K_i + U_i + W_{nc} = K_f + U_f$$

  • Mechanical energy is conserved only if $W_{nc} = 0$.
  • Friction and drag convert mechanical energy into thermal energy.

Quick Check

A roller coaster drops 20 m from rest. What is its speed at the bottom (no friction)?

$$mgh = \tfrac12 mv^2 \Rightarrow v=\sqrt{2gh} \approx 20\text{ m/s}.$$

$$P = \frac{W}{t}, \quad P = Fv \cos\theta$$

  • Power measures how fast energy is transferred.
  • High force or high speed → high power.

Quick Check

A 70 kg student climbs 3 m in 4 s. Estimate average power.

$$W = mgh = 70(9.8)(3) \approx 2058\text{ J}$$ $$P = \frac{2058}{4} \approx 515\text{ W}.$$
